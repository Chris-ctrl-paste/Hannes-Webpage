  • Iilahti Sans As a final project from Novia University of Applied
    Sciences I had the opportunity to challenge myself to design a
    typeface. The work is thoroughly described from both a personal,
    theoretical and a practical standpoint

    Research
    In the early stages of the project I did research on different
    classes of type, their use, history and characteristics, also how
    different letterforms are constructed and the importance of
    compensating for optical illusions when designing type.

    Concept
    At the age of 7 I spray painted my first graffiti painting, on a
    plywood board in my granddads garage, ever since then I’ve
    been drawing and painting letters in all kinds of shapes
    and forms. I’ve always felt that graffiti and typography have many
    similarities, even tough they are quite different from each others.
    In this project I have tried to merge my interest in graffiti with
    traditional type design to create a typeface that has a personal
    touch yet usable.

    Design
    The design process is described step by step. Starting with
    introducing the font editor software, followed by how and what
    letters are created together, describing the similarities and
    differences between letters, and lastly a brief description of how
    the tracking and kerning is adjusted for a typeface.

    Result
    Iilahti Sans is a low contrast geometric sans-serif typeface with
    playful features that comes in two weights, regular and bold.
    Iilahti Sans is best suited as a display typeface in big sizes.
    The final project is presented in a 124-page book printed in 10 copies.
